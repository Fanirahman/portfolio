December 8, 2023 Fanirahman M

Embarking on a journey through the digital marketplace, this portfolio project delves into the intricacies of an E-commerce Sales Report. Unveiling a treasure trove of insights, the exploration aims to decipher the purchasing patterns of users and discern the gender distribution among buyers. Through meticulous data cleaning and exploration, we unravel the narrative of online sales, seeking not only what users buy but also who they are. If you're eager to dive deeper, the full code and raw dataset are available on my GitHub.

The expedition begins with a comprehensive survey of the raw E-commerce Sales dataset. Every column, a piece of the puzzle waiting to be unraveled, as I meticulously inspect and analyze the vast expanse of data. This initial step lays the foundation for understanding the terrain and sets the course for our data exploration journey

As we delved deeper into the labyrinth of our E-commerce sales report, a peculiar sight caught my analytical eye ‚Äì a multitude of country names that seemed to have embarked from the less-trodden linguistic paths. In a bid to simplify our dataset and foster a universal language, I set my sights on the unsung heroes of our data - the country codes. üåê Armed with these concise and widely recognized identifiers, our dataset underwent a transformation. The convoluted country names gracefully stepped aside, making way for a more standardized and comprehensible representation. From Albania to Zimbabwe, the metamorphosis was underway.

In the intricate landscape of E-commerce data, clarity is paramount. To distill meaningful insights, I utilized SQL's DECLARE prompt, sculpting a refined tableau with 8-9 purposeful columns. Each column serves as a brushstroke on our canvas, facilitating in-depth analysis and visualization. In this orchestrated data symphony, special attention is bestowed upon avid buyers. Through the DECLARE magic, the "minProductBought" ensemble emerges, spotlighting countries where citizens exhibit a penchant for purchasing more than 10 products.
